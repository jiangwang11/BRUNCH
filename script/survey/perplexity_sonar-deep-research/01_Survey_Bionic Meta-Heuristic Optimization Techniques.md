# Bionic Meta-Heuristic Optimization Techniques: A Comprehensive Review of Contemporary Advances (2022-2025)

This comprehensive review examines the recent developments in bionic meta-heuristic optimization techniques, drawing from a systematic analysis of the most influential research published between 2022 and 2025. Bionic meta-heuristic algorithms, also known as nature-inspired or bio-inspired optimization techniques, leverage principles derived from natural systems including evolutionary processes, swarm behavior, foraging mechanisms, and predator-prey dynamics to solve complex, nonlinear, high-dimensional optimization problems that challenge traditional mathematical approaches[5]. The field has witnessed substantial advancement through hybrid approaches that combine multiple algorithmic paradigms, adaptive parameter tuning mechanisms, and sophisticated mutation strategies that enhance convergence properties while maintaining population diversity. This review identifies eight major algorithmic categories—evolutionary, swarm intelligence, physics-inspired, ecosystem and plant-based, predator-prey, neural-inspired, human-inspired, and hybrid approaches—and provides critical analysis of their theoretical foundations, practical implementations, and emerging applications across engineering, machine learning, medical diagnostics, and industrial optimization domains. Contemporary research demonstrates that hybrid metaheuristics significantly outperform standalone algorithms in handling multi-objective optimization, high-dimensional search spaces, and constrained real-world problems. Moreover, the integration of machine learning with evolutionary computation has emerged as a transformative research direction, enabling dynamic parameter adaptation and improved solution quality across diverse problem classes.

## Evolutionary Algorithms and Genetic Programming Approaches

Evolutionary algorithms represent one of the most mature and widely-studied categories of bio-inspired optimization, with roots extending back to Holland's foundational work on genetic algorithms in the 1970s. Genetic algorithms (GAs) remain central to contemporary research, operating through mechanisms of selection, crossover, and mutation that simulate biological evolution[10]. Recent advances have focused on overcoming fundamental limitations of traditional GAs, particularly addressing premature convergence in high-dimensional spaces and improving exploitation capabilities. The GAAPO algorithm introduced in 2024 demonstrates a sophisticated hybrid framework that integrates genetic algorithm principles with specialized prompt generation strategies, achieving superior generalization performance compared to single-method baselines through adaptive strategy weighting that dynamically allocates computational resources[7]. Differential Evolution (DE) algorithms have emerged as particularly effective alternatives to GAs, with the APDSDE variant incorporating adaptive parameter control and dual mutation strategies that improve both exploration and exploitation through non-linear population size reduction methods[15]. The EBJADE algorithm extends these advances through multi-population mechanisms that dynamically allocate mutation strategies based on performance metrics, incorporating elite solution neighborhoods from Estimation of Distribution Algorithms to enhance solution generation[18].

The theoretical understanding of evolutionary algorithms has advanced substantially through rigorous convergence analysis and stability assessment. Constriction-based Particle Swarm Optimization (CSPSO) with constriction factors demonstrates enhanced potential for balancing exploration and exploitation compared to standard PSO variants, with theoretical convergence guarantees derived from Markov process analysis and time-step dynamics[32]. Enhanced variants of genetic algorithms have shown particular promise in addressing concept drift in machine learning applications, where evolutionary mechanisms enable dynamic adaptation to shifting data distributions through population-based solution management[10]. These advances highlight a crucial trend toward mathematically grounded algorithm design that combines empirical effectiveness with theoretical convergence guarantees.

## Swarm Intelligence and Collective Behavior Algorithms

Swarm intelligence algorithms constitute the largest and most diverse category of contemporary bio-inspired optimization, with foundations in the collective behaviors observed in natural systems such as bird flocking, fish schooling, and insect colonies[3]. Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO) remains dominant within this category, with numerous variants addressing specific performance limitations. The Hybrid Strategy Particle Swarm Optimization (HSPSO) algorithm introduced in 2024 integrates adaptive weight adjustment mechanisms, reverse learning strategies, Cauchy mutation for enhanced global search capability, and Hook-Jeeves local optimization to achieve superior performance on high-dimensional benchmark functions[8]. HSPSO demonstrates consistently superior results across multiple CEC benchmark functions, with particularly strong performance on multimodal problems where standard PSO frequently converges prematurely. Constriction-based variants have proven effective in controlling velocity dynamics and preventing divergence while maintaining convergence properties[32].

Ant Colony Optimization (ACO) represents another mature swarm intelligence approach, with recent applications extending beyond traditional combinatorial optimization to address complex real-world problems including scheduling, feature selection, and network design[9][12]. The underlying pheromone-based communication mechanism has inspired numerous variants that employ adaptive strategies for balancing exploration and exploitation, with particular success demonstrated in multi-objective optimization contexts where colonies can maintain populations representing diverse Pareto-optimal solutions[24]. Contemporary research emphasizes hybrid approaches combining ACO with local search mechanisms and reinforcement learning components to improve convergence properties[43].

Firefly Algorithm (FA) and Cuckoo Search (CS) represent newer swarm intelligence approaches that have gained significant attention for their simplicity and effectiveness on diverse problem classes. The Hybrid FireFly Algorithm and Cuckoo Search (HFFACS) algorithm successfully combines firefly attraction mechanisms with cuckoo search Lévy flight capabilities, addressing the fundamental challenge of premature convergence in firefly algorithms through periodic injection of exploratory moves[19]. Experimental validation on six constrained optimization benchmarks and five engineering design problems demonstrates that HFFACS achieves optimal or near-optimal solutions within reasonable computational budgets while avoiding the stagnation issues that characterize standalone firefly implementations.

## Advanced Bio-Inspired Algorithms Inspired by Predatory and Migration Behavior

Recent algorithmic innovation has focused intensively on bio-inspired algorithms derived from predator-prey relationships and animal migration patterns, representing a departure from more conventional social insect models. The Whale Optimization Algorithm (WOA), inspired by the cooperative hunting behavior of humpback whales, has achieved remarkable success across diverse optimization domains including neural network training, feature selection, and engineering design optimization[13]. The WOA integrates spiral update mechanisms derived from whale hunting spirals with encirclement strategies, demonstrating advantages in solution precision and convergence speed compared to earlier swarm algorithms[13]. Recent enhancements to WOA incorporate quantum mechanics principles and Bohr atomic model theory, proposing WOAAD (Whale Optimization Algorithm based on Atom-like Structure Differential Evolution) that redefines whale hunting behavior as quantum mechanical processes with electron orbital dynamics[13]. This quantum-inspired variant accelerates convergence, improves optimization precision, and effectively escapes local optima through crossover operations and scout bee strategies.

The Grey Wolf Optimization (GWO) algorithm, inspired by the social hierarchy and cooperative hunting behaviors of gray wolves, has emerged as a powerful competitor to PSO and GA approaches. The improved GWO (IGWO) algorithm published in 2024 addresses critical limitations of standard GWO through multi-strategy fusion incorporating lens imaging reverse learning for enhanced population coverage, nonlinear convergence strategies significantly improving exploration efficiency, and novel position update mechanisms enabling superior local optima avoidance[14]. Comprehensive benchmarking across 23 standard functions, 15 CEC2014 problems, and two engineering design challenges demonstrates IGWO's balanced exploration and exploitation capabilities with consistent advantages over competing state-of-the-art algorithms including PSO, GA, and other advanced variants.

The Aquila Optimizer (AO), based on the hunting and searching behavior of Aquila eagles, addresses high-dimensional complex optimization through multi-strategy enhancement mechanisms. The MSAO (Multi-Strategy Aquila Optimizer) incorporates random sub-dimension update mechanisms for enhanced exploration in high-dimensional spaces, integrating memory and dream-sharing strategies from Dream Optimization Algorithm to achieve balance between global exploration and local exploitation[25]. Experimental results on CEC benchmark functions demonstrate MSAO's outstanding performance, achieving best results on 55-72% of test functions depending on dimensionality. The Sterna Migration Algorithm (StMA), inspired by bird migration patterns, introduces novel multi-cluster sectoral approaches for efficient bionic engineering applications[4].

## Hybrid and Multi-Strategy Meta-Heuristic Approaches

Contemporary research demonstrates overwhelmingly that hybrid algorithms combining diverse optimization strategies significantly outperform single-method approaches across complex problem classes. The integration of multiple algorithms within unified frameworks enables dynamic strategy selection based on problem characteristics and evolutionary progress, with substantial empirical evidence supporting this architectural approach. A particularly successful paradigm combines evolutionary algorithms with swarm intelligence components, exemplified by approaches integrating PSO with GA mechanisms (PSO-GA hybrids) that leverage global search capabilities of PSO with local exploitation properties of genetic operators[5][8].

The Dipper Throated Optimizer (DTO) combined with Polar Rose Search (PRS) in agricultural water quality prediction demonstrates effective integration of bio-inspired exploration mechanisms with systematic mathematical search strategies[21]. This DTO+PRS hybrid synergistically leverages exploitation strengths of DTO with global exploration properties of PRS, achieving superior convergence speeds and solution quality in feature selection and hyperparameter optimization tasks. The framework's success in agricultural applications validates the broader principle that domain-specific hybrid approaches can substantially improve both computational efficiency and solution quality.

Harris Hawk Optimizer (HHO) variants demonstrate the effectiveness of multiple hybridization strategies, with seven novel variants (HHO-ADAP, HHO-CHAOS, HHO-Elite, HHO-GA, HHO-Inertia, HHO-PSO, HHO-ULTRA) achieving up to 35% faster convergence and solving optimization problems to fitness values as low as 10⁻²¹⁶ compared to classical baselines achieving only 10⁻⁴² to 10⁻⁴⁷[42]. The success of HHO-PSO and HHO-Elite variants particularly highlights how cross-algorithmic integration of predator-prey dynamics with swarm intelligence produces demonstrably superior results on both benchmark functions and constrained engineering problems. These advances establish clear empirical superiority of strategic hybridization approaches over traditional single-algorithm implementations.

Slime Mould Algorithm (SMA) enhancements incorporating Laplace logistic sine mapping, adaptive t-distribution elite mutation mechanisms, and ranking-based dynamic learning strategies demonstrate how sophisticated mutation operators and adaptive selection can substantially improve algorithm performance[39]. The enhanced SMA (EESMA) achieves superior balance between global exploration and local exploitation through integrated mutation strategies, with particularly strong results on complex multimodal benchmark functions where traditional SMA convergence characteristics are insufficient.

## Applications in Medical Diagnosis and Disease Detection

Bio-inspired optimization algorithms have achieved substantial impact in medical and healthcare applications, with particular success demonstrated in feature selection for deep learning models used in disease diagnosis. The comprehensive integration of bio-inspired optimization techniques with deep learning models for medical diagnostics represents a major research direction, with genetic algorithms, particle swarm optimization, and ant colony optimization all demonstrating effectiveness in identifying essential features and reducing dimensionality while maintaining or improving diagnostic accuracy[2]. Bio-inspired feature selection mechanisms significantly decrease computational costs and model redundancy while improving robustness for handling incomplete and noisy biomedical data typical of real-world clinical applications.

Breast cancer detection from mammography and histopathological images employs GA and PSO for optimizing deep learning architectures and parameters, with demonstrable improvements in both detection sensitivity and specificity[2]. Lung cancer detection similarly benefits from bio-inspired optimization, with GAs enhancing nodule detection architectures and PSO tuning parameters to improve discrimination between tumor subtypes from chest radiographs and CT scans. ACO contributes through feature selection mechanisms that identify informative diagnostic features from high-dimensional medical imaging data. The consistent success of these approaches across diverse cancer types indicates broader applicability of bio-inspired optimization to medical imaging and diagnostics generally.

Hybrid models combining PSO and ACO for cardiovascular disease prediction from clinical datasets demonstrate superior accuracy compared to single optimization approaches when integrated with machine learning classifiers including Random Forest and Support Vector Machines[24]. The synergistic combination of PSO's continuous optimization properties with ACO's discrete feature selection capabilities provides complementary strengths for addressing the distinct challenges of cardiovascular disease prediction from complex clinical data.

## Parameter Tuning, Neural Architecture Search, and Deep Learning Optimization

Bio-inspired optimization has emerged as the dominant approach for neural architecture search (NAS), hyperparameter optimization, and deep learning model refinement, replacing computational-intensive grid search and random search methods with intelligent population-based optimization. The NAS field demonstrates clear evolution from simple reinforcement learning approaches toward evolutionary algorithms and gradient-based methods that more efficiently navigate high-dimensional architecture spaces[20][49]. Evolutionary algorithms for NAS achieve computational advantages through sampled training and node inheritance strategies that evaluate offspring individuals without full retraining, substantially reducing overall computational requirements while maintaining architecture quality.

GAAPO (Genetic Algorithm Applied to Prompt Optimization) demonstrates effectiveness of genetic algorithms for optimizing prompts used in large language models, achieving superior validation performance and better generalization than baseline methods through hybrid integration of multiple specialized prompt generation strategies[7]. The framework's modular architecture enabling flexible adaptation to various tasks while maintaining optimization effectiveness exemplifies contemporary trends toward algorithmic versatility and task-adaptive strategy selection.

Transfer learning optimization through BioTune, an evolutionary adaptive fine-tuning approach, selectively optimizes layer freezing decisions and learning rate configurations across diverse datasets and CNN architectures[48]. Comprehensive benchmarking across nine image classification datasets demonstrates BioTune's superiority in balancing accuracy improvements with computational efficiency, achieving particularly substantial gains on fine-grained classification tasks and datasets distant from the source domain of pre-training.

## Real-World Engineering Applications and Constrained Optimization

Bio-inspired optimization algorithms have achieved significant practical impact in solving complex constrained engineering problems where traditional optimization methods prove insufficient. The White-faced Capuchin Optimizer (WCO) represents novel bionic metaheuristic approaches for solving diverse optimization problems[1]. Enhanced Aquila Optimizer algorithms incorporating restart strategies, opposition-based learning, and chaotic local search overcome slow convergence and local optima trapping issues in complex engineering design problems[28]. Multi-strategy enhancement of algorithms continues to represent the dominant research trend, with particular success demonstrated on CEC benchmark test suites and real-world engineering challenges including pressure vessel design, welded beam optimization, gear train design, and spring design problems.

Constrained multi-objective design optimization in systems engineering increasingly employs bio-inspired algorithms to explore high-dimensional design spaces involving competing objectives[60]. Active learning approaches combined with evolutionary algorithms enable efficient discovery of Pareto-optimal solutions for automotive systems engineering applications involving complex nonlinear relationships between design parameters and performance objectives. The successful application of metaheuristics to systems engineering design demonstrates capabilities substantially exceeding traditional simulation-based optimization approaches, particularly when handling numerous design parameters and competing design goals.

## Emerging Trends and Future Directions

Contemporary research in bio-inspired optimization reveals several transformative trends that will likely dominate the field through 2025 and beyond. The integration of machine learning with metaheuristic algorithms represents perhaps the most significant research direction, with emerging work exploring reinforcement learning-based algorithm configuration, neural network-based solution prediction, and machine learning-guided parameter adaptation[43][46]. This convergence acknowledges that different problems benefit from distinct algorithmic strategies, motivating development of meta-learning approaches that select and adapt algorithms to specific problem characteristics.

Quantum-inspired classical computing represents another frontier, with quantum-inspired evolutionary optimization (QIEO) algorithms demonstrating remarkable performance improvements over classical genetic algorithms on highly nonlinear and non-convex benchmark problems[44][47]. Quantum bilevel optimization algorithms for network design problems highlight how quantum computing concepts can inform and improve classical metaheuristic design even in purely classical computing contexts. The demonstrated speedups of 3-4x for complex optimization problems establish quantum inspiration as a promising research direction.

Adaptive and self-tuning algorithms represent a third major trend, with contemporary research emphasizing dynamic parameter adjustment during algorithm execution based on search progress, population diversity, and fitness landscape characteristics[50][53]. The Adaptive Dynamic Particle Swarm Optimization enhanced with Guided Whale Optimization Algorithm demonstrates how adaptive mechanisms can overcome fundamental algorithm limitations including premature convergence and inadequate exploration in complex high-dimensional spaces. This trend reflects recognition that fixed parameter settings cannot be universally optimal across diverse problem classes and algorithm stages.

Multi-objective and many-objective optimization with more than three competing objectives increasingly dominates industrial applications, motivating algorithm development specifically addressing Pareto frontier approximation, solution diversity maintenance, and computational scalability in many-dimensional objective spaces[27]. Contemporary research documents clear advantages of hybrid metaheuristics in handling multiple competing objectives compared to single-algorithm approaches, with successful applications in modern Industry 4.0 and 5.0 manufacturing contexts involving competing goals for makespan minimization, energy efficiency, production cost reduction, and human-machine collaboration.

## Conclusion

Bionic meta-heuristic optimization techniques have achieved substantial maturity and practical impact across diverse application domains, from medical diagnostics to engineering design and deep learning optimization. The field has transitioned from research focused on individual algorithm development toward sophisticated hybrid approaches that strategically combine complementary algorithmic paradigms to address specific problem characteristics. Contemporary advances emphasize adaptive parameter control, multi-strategy integration, and dynamic algorithm selection based on problem features and evolutionary progress. The overwhelming empirical evidence demonstrates consistent superiority of hybrid metaheuristics over single-algorithm approaches across benchmark functions and real-world problems.

Future research will likely emphasize integration with machine learning for meta-algorithm selection and parameter tuning, quantum-inspired approaches providing provable performance advantages, and adaptive mechanisms enabling algorithms to dynamically adjust strategies during execution. The convergence of evolutionary computation with deep learning and neural architecture search represents a particularly promising direction, with opportunities for substantial performance improvements through intelligent algorithm-problem matching and dynamic adaptation. Constrained multi-objective optimization addressing realistic engineering problems with numerous competing objectives will increasingly dominate application research, particularly in sustainable manufacturing and systems engineering contexts.

The maturation of bio-inspired optimization creates opportunities for practitioners to move beyond simple algorithm application toward sophisticated problem-aware approaches that select and adapt algorithms to specific challenge characteristics. This shift from generic algorithm development to specialized problem-solving approaches, combined with integration of machine learning and quantum inspiration, will likely define the next major phase of research advancement. The field demonstrates remarkable vitality with continuous algorithmic innovation, expanding application domains, and increasingly rigorous theoretical analysis establishing convergence guarantees and performance bounds for diverse algorithm variants and problem classes.
