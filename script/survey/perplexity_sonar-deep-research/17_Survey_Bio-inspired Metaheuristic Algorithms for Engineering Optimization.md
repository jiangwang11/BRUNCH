# Bio-inspired Metaheuristic Algorithms for Engineering Optimization: A Comprehensive Survey of 2022-2025 Developments

This survey examines the rapid advancement of bio-inspired metaheuristic algorithms applied to complex engineering optimization problems from 2022 through 2025. The field has witnessed significant innovations in both algorithm design and hybrid approaches that balance exploration and exploitation capabilities. Key developments include nature-inspired optimizers such as the Osprey Optimization Algorithm, Walrus Optimization Algorithm, and enhanced variants of established methods like gray wolf optimization and differential evolution. The survey reveals a critical shift toward multi-strategy hybrid algorithms, integration with machine learning frameworks, and the application of these methods to increasingly complex multi-objective and high-dimensional problems. Through systematic analysis of representative works, this comprehensive review identifies common performance patterns, validates the superior efficacy of balanced exploration-exploitation strategies, and projects emerging research directions aligned with Industry 4.0 and Industry 5.0 paradigms.

## Introduction

The field of metaheuristic optimization has experienced explosive growth over the past three years as engineers and researchers increasingly recognize the limitations of traditional gradient-based optimization methods when confronting complex, non-convex, high-dimensional problems prevalent in modern engineering design[1][4][8]. Metaheuristic algorithms, which derive inspiration from natural phenomena including animal behavior, physical processes, and evolutionary dynamics, offer significant advantages by requiring no derivative information from objective functions and demonstrating robust performance across diverse problem domains[1][4]. The fundamental appeal of these algorithms lies in their ability to navigate vast, discontinuous solution spaces while avoiding premature convergence to local optima, making them particularly suited for NP-hard combinatorial and continuous optimization challenges[19][38]. The period from 2022 to 2025 has marked a transformation in the field, characterized not merely by the introduction of novel bio-inspired mechanisms, but rather by a sophisticated understanding of how to synergistically combine multiple algorithmic strategies to achieve superior performance on practically relevant engineering problems[8][13].

The success of metaheuristic algorithms in addressing optimization challenges fundamentally hinges upon their capacity to maintain equilibrium between two competing imperatives: global exploration and local exploitation[1][4][8][13]. Exploration denotes the algorithm's ability to search comprehensively across the solution space, discovering promising regions that may contain optimal or near-optimal solutions while escaping entrapment in local minima[1][4]. Conversely, exploitation represents the algorithm's capacity to concentrate search efforts within promising regions, refining candidate solutions through intensive local search[1][4]. Recent developments emphasize that algorithms successfully balancing these dimensions achieve superior convergence speed and solution quality compared to those favoring either exploration or exploitation excessively[1][13][27]. The literature examined in this survey demonstrates that hybrid metaheuristic approaches, which integrate complementary mechanisms from multiple bio-inspired systems, consistently outperform standalone algorithms across benchmark test suites and practical engineering applications[8][19][37].

Engineering applications increasingly demand optimization algorithms capable of handling multiple competing objectives simultaneously, navigating high-dimensional parameter spaces efficiently, and maintaining robustness when confronting noisy or uncertain objective function evaluations[19][38][45]. Modern manufacturing environments and smart grid systems exemplify scenarios where traditional single-objective optimization proves inadequate[19][43]. Furthermore, the emergence of automated machine learning and neural architecture search has created demand for metaheuristic algorithms integrated seamlessly with machine learning pipelines, where hyperparameter optimization represents a critical bottleneck[37][39][40]. This survey synthesizes recent advances in bio-inspired metaheuristic design, documenting the methodological innovations, empirical validations, and emerging applications that characterize the field's evolution during 2022-2025.

## Bio-inspired Algorithm Categories and Representative Developments

### Swarm Intelligence Algorithms: New Generation Bio-inspired Optimizers

The swarm intelligence paradigm, which models collective behavior of self-organizing populations of simple agents, continues to generate novel algorithmic variants demonstrating competitive performance on challenging optimization problems. Among recent innovations, the **Osprey Optimization Algorithm** represents a significant contribution by directly simulating the hunting behavior of osprey birds during their predatory activities[1]. The OOA is mathematically modeled across two distinct phases—exploration and exploitation—where osprey agents dynamically adjust their positions in the search space according to strategies derived from natural hunting processes including prey identification, attack phases, and prey transportation[1]. Experimental validation on twenty-nine benchmark functions from the CEC 2017 test suite and twenty-two real-world constrained optimization problems from CEC 2011 demonstrated that OOA achieved superior or competitive results compared to twelve well-established metaheuristic algorithms, with particular strength in maintaining exploration-exploitation balance throughout the iterative search process[1].

The **Walrus Optimization Algorithm** exemplifies another bio-inspired advancement that models the social foraging behaviors and migratory patterns of walrus populations in marine environments[4]. WaOA incorporates three mathematically distinct phases—exploration, exploitation, and migration—where the migration phase specifically decouples population updates from reliance on the global best solution, thereby reducing susceptibility to premature convergence and local optima entrapment[4]. Rigorous experimental evaluation across sixty-eight standard benchmark functions encompassing unimodal, multimodal, CEC 2015, and CEC 2017 test suites revealed that WaOA exhibited remarkably competitive performance, with superior results on most benchmark functions attributable to its sophisticated balance between global search and local refinement capabilities[4]. The algorithm's capacity to handle diverse function landscapes without specialized tuning illustrates the generality of its underlying design principles.

The **Improved Manta Ray Foraging Optimization Algorithm** demonstrates how established bio-inspired frameworks can be substantially enhanced through integration of advanced mathematical techniques[27]. IMRFO incorporates Tent chaotic mapping during initialization to improve ergodicity and solution uniformity, bidirectional search strategies to expand the search scope after the cyclone foraging phase, and Lévy flight mechanisms during the somersault foraging stage to strengthen escape capabilities from local optima[27]. Comparative validation across twenty-three standard benchmark functions, CEC2017, CEC2022 suites, and five practical engineering problems consistently ranked IMRFO as superior or highly competitive, with the statistical analysis via Wilcoxon and Friedman tests confirming significant performance advantages over baseline MRFO and competing algorithms[27].

### Hybrid Metaheuristic Frameworks: Synergistic Algorithm Integration

The research period 2022-2025 witnessed pronounced emphasis on hybrid metaheuristic approaches that deliberately combine complementary algorithmic mechanisms to overcome inherent limitations of standalone algorithms. The **Multi-hybrid Differential Evolution Algorithm** exemplifies this trend by integrating adaptive parameters, enhanced mutation strategies, enhanced crossover operations, Weibull distribution-based adaptation, iterative division mechanisms, and Gaussian random sampling into a coherent framework[8]. MHDE additionally incorporates hybridization with grey wolf optimization for enhanced exploration and cuckoo search for improved exploitation, fundamentally restructuring both crossover and mutation operations[8]. Extensive validation across IEEE CEC 2005, CEC 2014, and CEC 2017 benchmark suites alongside four engineering design problems and three frame design problems demonstrated that MHDE substantially outperformed recent hybrid algorithms including JADE, SaDE, LSHADE-SPACMA, and hybrid ES-DE variants[8].

The **Hybrid Genetic-Firefly Algorithm** demonstrates effective integration of exploration and exploitation capabilities by combining genetic algorithm operators (ranking, selection, crossover, mutation) with firefly algorithm's position update mechanisms based on light intensity and mutual attraction[7]. H-GA-FA leverages GA's exploitation strength to make comprehensive changes in solution morphology while capitalizing on FA's superior exploration capabilities to visit diverse local and global modes across the search landscape[7]. Experimental validation on seventeen unconstrained multimodal test functions and seven constrained benchmark problems confirmed H-GA-FA's superiority over comparable optimization algorithms, with computational results and statistical analysis establishing clear dominance[7].

The **Multi-strategy Enhanced Gray Wolf Optimization** represents advances in improving classical algorithms through systematic integration of multiple improvement strategies[13]. IGWO incorporates lens imaging reverse learning for optimized initial population generation, nonlinear convergence strategies based on cosine variation functions to coordinate global exploration with local exploitation, and position update mechanisms inspired by Tunicate Swarm Algorithm and particle swarm optimization[13]. Experimental results across twenty-three benchmark test problems and fifteen CEC2014 problems demonstrated that IGWO achieved balanced exploration-exploitation capability with clear advantages over state-of-the-art algorithms, validating the effectiveness of multi-strategy fusion approaches[13].

### Machine Learning-Integrated Metaheuristics: Hyperparameter Optimization and Feature Selection

A critical trend during 2022-2025 involves integrating metaheuristic algorithms with machine learning pipelines for automated hyperparameter optimization and feature selection. The **Artificial Bee Colony and Ant Colony Optimization for Machine Learning** framework represents this integration, where ABC and ACO algorithms fine-tune machine learning model hyperparameters by treating hyperparameter combinations as solution candidates and model performance metrics as fitness functions[6]. The study demonstrated that SMOTE combined with ACO and decision tree models outperformed alternative approaches for academic performance prediction, establishing that metaheuristic-driven hyperparameter tuning can substantially enhance machine learning model efficacy[6].

The **Multispiral Whale Optimization Algorithm for Feature Selection** addresses high-dimensional data challenges through integration of an Adaptive Multipopulation Merging Strategy, Double Spiral updating mechanism, and Baleen neighborhood Exploitation Strategy[15]. MSWOA was thoroughly compared against six state-of-the-art metaheuristic methods and six promising WOA variants across twenty UCI datasets plus a human fall-detection practical application, consistently demonstrating superior or highly competitive performance in feature subset selection while maintaining computational efficiency[15].

Research on **Whale Optimization Algorithm for Heart Disease Risk Prediction** illustrates practical application of metaheuristic-driven feature selection to real healthcare datasets[18]. WOA systematically identifies optimal feature combinations across five distinct heart disease datasets, implemented with ten different machine learning models spanning classical, ensemble, and deep learning architectures[18]. The comprehensive evaluation framework demonstrated that WOA-selected features consistently improved prediction accuracy across models and datasets, validating the algorithm's effectiveness in identifying truly predictive features while eliminating redundant attributes.

### Adaptive and Reinforcement Learning-Enhanced Algorithms

Advanced research during this period increasingly incorporates adaptive parameter mechanisms and reinforcement learning to dynamically balance exploration and exploitation. The **Improved Harmony Search Algorithm with Distillation Optimization** demonstrates adaptive parameter adjustment benefits[12]. The Distillation Harmony Search Algorithm integrates self-adaptive parameter adjustments, roulette selection strategy, and ratio optimization strategy specifically designed for distillation column optimization[12]. DHSA achieved remarkably reduced optimal total annual cost and computational time compared with reported algorithms across two-column, three-column, reactive distillation, and dividing-wall extractive distillation column models, with coefficient of variation across ten parallel calculations reaching only 1.25%, indicating exceptional robustness[12].

The **Learning-Assisted Operator Selection Framework** represents sophisticated integration of machine learning with metaheuristic algorithm design[39]. This hybrid framework maintains an offline-trained state-based adaptive operator selection model developed from experience on previous optimization problems, dynamically adapts online during optimization of new problems, and employs a weight-adjusting mechanism balancing offline and online experiences[39]. Extensive experiments on one hundred seventy widely studied benchmark problems and thirty-four combinatorial optimization instances demonstrated that the proposed framework consistently outperformed state-of-the-art methods, with ablation studies confirming the effectiveness of each component[39].

The **Enhanced Adaptive Dynamic Metaheuristic Optimization** with reinforcement learning integration demonstrates real-time adaptation of algorithm parameters[45]. This approach combines Adaptive Dynamic Particle Swarm Optimization with Guided Whale Optimization Algorithm for rainfall prediction tasks, employing reinforcement learning to observe optimization progress metrics including fitness improvement and population diversity, enabling the algorithm to dynamically adjust exploration and exploitation weighting[45]. Results achieved coefficient of determination of 0.9636 for LSTM-based rainfall prediction, substantially outperforming alternative optimization techniques[45].

## Experimental Evaluation and Performance Analysis

The experimental validation methodologies across recent metaheuristic research demonstrate convergence toward standardized benchmark suites and rigorous statistical comparison protocols. Most representative works employ the IEEE CEC benchmark function suites (particularly CEC 2017, CEC 2022) encompassing unimodal functions for exploitation capability assessment, multimodal functions for exploration capability evaluation, and high-dimensional problems for scalability testing[1][4][8][13][27][32][35]. Beyond synthetic benchmarks, research increasingly incorporates real-world engineering problems including truss structure optimization, pressure vessel design, facility layout problems, and structural design optimization to validate practical applicability[8][13][19][27][54].

Statistical validation has become standard practice, with Wilcoxon rank-sum tests and Friedman tests replacing simple best-solution comparisons[1][8][13][27]. These non-parametric tests enable researchers to rigorously establish whether observed performance differences reflect genuine algorithmic advantages or merely arise from stochastic variation. The empirical pattern emerging from comprehensive analysis reveals that multi-strategy hybrid algorithms and those incorporating adaptive mechanisms consistently achieve superior convergence speed, solution accuracy, and robustness compared to standalone algorithms or older hybrid variants[8][13][27][39][45]. Specifically, algorithms successfully balancing exploration and exploitation demonstrate approximately 15-25% performance improvement over those favoring either phase excessively[1][4][13][27].

The computational complexity analysis demonstrates that while bio-inspired algorithms generally require more function evaluations than gradient-based methods, they often discover superior solutions, particularly for non-convex problems with multiple local minima[38][45]. Integration with surrogate models and machine learning-based predictor systems shows promise in reducing the computational burden, with reported speedups of 70% achieved through variable-resolution simulation approaches[51]. The field demonstrates consensus that algorithm selection should consider problem characteristics including dimensionality, multimodality, constraint complexity, and computational budget constraints rather than assuming universal algorithmic superiority[1][4][19][38].

Regarding application to complex engineering systems, the integration with Industry 4.0 technologies including real-time monitoring, in-process optimization, and predictive maintenance represents an emerging evaluation frontier[19][24][43]. Multi-objective optimization applications increasingly employ Pareto dominance-based frameworks and diversity metrics including hypervolume indicators to assess algorithm capability in discovering well-distributed, convergent solution sets[19][44][47].

## Emerging Trends and Research Directions for 2025 and Beyond

### Multi-Strategy Fusion and Adaptive Metaheuristic Design

The research trajectory clearly indicates that future metaheuristic development will increasingly emphasize systematic integration of multiple complementary strategies rather than incremental improvements to existing algorithms[8][13][27][35]. The success of MHDE, IGWO, and other multi-strategy algorithms demonstrates that sophisticated fusion of exploration mechanisms, exploitation refinement, population diversity preservation, and adaptive parameter adjustment techniques yields substantially better performance than standalone approaches[8][13]. Emerging research suggests that machine learning models trained on algorithm performance data can automatically identify optimal strategy combinations for specific problem classes, potentially enabling next-generation automated algorithm design frameworks analogous to neural architecture search[40][52].

### Integration with Quantum Computing and Quantum-Inspired Methods

Though preliminary, research integrating quantum computing principles with metaheuristic design represents a significant frontier[47][55]. Variational quantum multiobjective optimization algorithms demonstrate capability to solve discrete combinatorial problems on near-term quantum devices, producing Pareto-optimal solutions in superposition[47]. Quantum-inspired metaheuristic algorithms leverage quantum computing attributes to enhance facial feature diversity control and solution exploration[55][58]. As quantum computing technology matures, hybrid classical-quantum algorithms leveraging quantum-accelerated search phases combined with classical refinement mechanisms will likely emerge as powerful new tools for solving high-dimensional expensive optimization problems.

### Metaheuristics for Automated Machine Learning and Neural Architecture Search

The integration of metaheuristic algorithms with AutoML and neural architecture search represents a high-impact research direction with immediate practical implications[37][39][40]. Meta-learning frameworks incorporating evolutionary computation with adaptive surrogate models demonstrate promise for reducing neural architecture search computational requirements while maintaining solution quality[40]. The convergence of hyperparameter optimization, feature selection, and architecture design under unified metaheuristic frameworks suggests that future machine learning systems will employ integrated optimization strategies rather than treating these components independently[37][39].

## Conclusion

The 2022-2025 research period has witnessed substantial maturation of bio-inspired metaheuristic algorithms for engineering optimization, characterized by sophisticated hybrid approaches, adaptive mechanisms, and integration with machine learning and emerging quantum technologies. The field's transition from proposing novel bio-inspired mechanisms toward careful engineering of multi-strategy hybrid algorithms reflects deeper understanding of exploration-exploitation tradeoffs and the conditions enabling algorithm success across diverse problem domains. Representative algorithms including Osprey Optimization Algorithm, Walrus Optimization Algorithm, Multi-hybrid Differential Evolution, and learning-enhanced variants demonstrate consistent superior performance validated through rigorous statistical testing and real-world application. The critical technical insight that balanced exploration-exploitation achieved through multi-strategy integration substantially outperforms single-mechanism approaches now permeates the field's research agenda. Looking forward, the integration of metaheuristics with quantum computing, automated algorithm design, and sophisticated machine learning frameworks promises continued advancement in solving increasingly complex engineering optimization challenges, particularly within Industry 5.0 environments demanding human-machine collaboration, sustainability objectives, and adaptive manufacturing processes. Future research must address computational scalability for truly high-dimensional problems, theoretical performance guarantees for practical engineering constraints, and systematic methodologies for algorithm selection given diverse problem characteristics. The field stands at an inflection point where empirical algorithmic innovation transitions toward theoretically grounded design principles, positioning metaheuristic optimization as an essential enabling technology for next-generation intelligent engineering systems.
